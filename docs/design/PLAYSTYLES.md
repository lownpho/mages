# Playstyles

How the equipment and spell systems combine into distinct playstyles. Each archetype emerges
from the four-stat budget: **skill** (offense), **HP** (passive defense), **speed** (active
defense), **mana** (sustain). Every build invests differently across these four axes, creating
a different combat rhythm and Focus pattern.

---

## The stat budget

Every equipment choice is a tradeoff. You have three gear slots (weapon, hat, robe) and four
spell slots. No item has negative stats, so the cost of any choice is the stats you *didn't*
pick.

| Stat | Role | What it buys |
|------|------|-------------|
| Skill | Burst offense | Damage scaling on weapons and instant spells |
| Mana | Sustained offense / sustain | Fuel for channeled spells (Laser, Barrier); more shots before Focus |
| HP | Passive defense | Survive hits, stay in the fight |
| Speed | Active defense | Outrun threats, create safe Focus windows |

There are two offensive paths: **skill scales burst damage** (instant spells, weapon shots),
**mana scales sustained damage** (channeled spells with high base damage, low skill scaling).
A glass cannon stacks skill because each Fireball hits harder. A channeler stacks mana because
they can Laser for 20 seconds instead of 5.

The core defensive choice: **HP lets you take hits, speed lets you avoid them.** Both keep you
alive, but they create fundamentally different combat rhythms and Focus patterns.

Utility (Blink, Haste, Frost, Heal) benefits all builds equally — low or no scaling, moderate
mana cost.

## Sniper

*Kill everything before it reaches you.*

| Slot | Item | Key stats |
|------|------|-----------|
| Weapon | Wand | 1.0 skill scaling, homing, fast fire rate, low mana cost |
| Hat | Hat | Pure skill (+10 to +150) |
| Robe | Robe | Pure mana (+10 to +130) |

**Stat profile:** Max skill, max mana, base HP, base speed.

**How it plays:** You deal the highest per-shot damage in the game and have the mana pool to
sustain it. The Wand's homing and fast fire rate mean you're constantly outputting damage. With
full skill stacking, even basic weapon shots hit like spells. You don't need to dodge if
everything dies first.

**Focus pattern:** Infrequent — Wand is mana-efficient (1-3 cost), and your deep mana pool
means you can fire many times before running dry. But when you do Focus, it's dangerous. Base
100 HP means a single Skitter rush during Focus can cripple you.

**Spell picks:** Double down on damage. Fireball and Piercing Lights for burst. Chain Lightning
for cheap filler. Blink as a panic escape since you can't take hits.

**Threat:** Husks. Their 50 HP requires sustained fire, and while you're focused on burning one
down, other enemies close in on your fragile HP pool.

**Fantasy:** Glass cannon. The mage who ends fights in seconds or dies trying.

## Kite Mage

*Never stop moving. Never get hit.*

| Slot | Item | Key stats |
|------|------|-----------|
| Weapon | Wand | Homing (fire while moving), fast rate |
| Hat | Headband | Skill + speed (+7/+5 to +120/+50) |
| Robe | Vesture | Balanced stats, including speed (+0/+0/+0/+0 to +50/+50/+40/+15) |

**Stat profile:** High speed, good skill, modest HP and mana.

**How it plays:** With Headband + Vesture speed bonuses, you outrun every enemy in the game
including Skitters (80 speed). The Wand's homing lets you deal damage while running — you never
have to stop moving to aim. Combat is constant motion: circle enemies, fire into the pack, stay
ahead of everything.

**Focus pattern:** Positional. You create safe distance by outrunning threats, then Focus in
the gap. Focus isn't risky because nothing can catch you — the tradeoff is that you deal less
damage than a Sniper during the time you spend running. Speed buys safety but costs DPS uptime.

**Spell picks:** Mobility and fire-and-forget. Chain Lightning (cheap, cast while running),
Tornado (drop in your wake, enemies run through it), Blink (snap to a new position if cornered).
Haste is redundant — you're already fast.

**Threat:** Rangers. You can outrun melee, but Ranger projectiles don't care about your speed.
Rangers force you to close distance or find cover, breaking your kiting loop.

**Fantasy:** Hit and run. The mage who's always three steps ahead.

## Tank

*Walk into the fight. Walk out of the fight.*

| Slot | Item | Key stats |
|------|------|-----------|
| Weapon | Rune | Shotgun, short range, +HP (+5 to +80) |
| Hat | Hood | Skill + HP + mana (+7/+3/+3 to +120/+30/+30) |
| Robe | Mantle | HP + mana (+10/+0 to +130/+50) |

**Stat profile:** Max HP (up to 340), decent skill, decent mana, base speed.

**How it plays:** You have more than three times the HP of a Sniper. The Rune's short range (4)
means you fight at point blank — walk into enemies, shotgun them, absorb their attacks. You
don't dodge, you don't kite. You stand your ground because you can afford to.

**Focus pattern:** Frequent and bold. Rune costs 2-6 mana per shot with a 2-second fire rate,
so you burn mana in bursts and need to Focus often. But with 340 HP, you can Focus *in the
middle of enemies* and survive. Focus isn't a retreat — it's a reload.

**Spell picks:** Sustain and crowd control. Barrier (damage redirected to mana — your massive
HP pool becomes a mana battery), Heal (top off your huge health pool), Summon Golem (another
body in the melee), Curse Zone or Frost (punish enemies that cluster around you).

**Threat:** Mana starvation. Only +80 mana from Hood + Mantle. With Rune costing 6/shot at
ultimate tier, you get roughly 13 shots before empty. You're Focusing constantly, and if
enemies interrupt your Focus faster than you recover, you're a tank with no ammo.

**Fantasy:** Battlemage. The mage who fights like a warrior.

## Channeler

*Your mana pool is your weapon. Keep the beam running.*

| Slot | Item | Key stats |
|------|------|-----------|
| Weapon | Staff | High base damage, slow fire rate (0.5), 0.5 skill scaling, **+mana** |
| Hat | Hood | Skill + HP + mana (+7/+3/+3 to +120/+30/+30) |
| Robe | Robe | Pure mana (+10 to +130) |

**Stat profile:** Max mana (up to 225 with Staff+Robe+Hood), decent skill, decent HP, base speed.

**How it plays:** You have the deepest mana pool in the game. Your primary damage comes from
channeled spells — Laser, Charge Blast, Barrier — which have high base damage and low skill
scaling. You don't need skill gear because these spells hit hard on base values alone. What
you need is mana to keep channeling. The Staff's +mana feeds your channels, and its high base
damage (90-120 at top tiers) means your weapon still hits hard between channels without skill
stacking.

A Channeler's Laser 2 at 225 mana: 25 + 130×0.3 = 64 DPS for 22.5 seconds = 1440 total
damage. Compare to a Sniper's Fireball: 15 + 160×2 = 335 burst, then wait 8 seconds for
cooldown. Different paths to damage — burst vs. sustain.

**Focus pattern:** Long and frequent. Channels drain mana fast (10-20/sec), so you alternate
between channeling and Focusing. The Staff's 2-second fire rate creates natural Focus windows
between shots. Your deep mana pool means each Focus session recovers a smaller percentage of
your pool — you Focus more often but with purpose, always building toward the next channel.

**Spell picks:** Channeled spells are core. Laser (sustained beam, signature spell), Barrier
(damage → mana, defensive channel), Charge Blast (burst channel). Fourth slot is utility:
Blink for escape, Tornado for area denial, Healing Idol for sustain.

**Threat:** Skitters. Your Staff fires slowly and your channels root you — Skitters exploit
both. A Skitter rushing you mid-Laser forces you to drop the channel (wasting the mana already
spent) or eat damage. You need Barrier or Blink to handle interrupts.

**Fantasy:** Sustained caster. The mage who opens a beam and doesn't stop until everything is
gone.

## Summoner

*Let others do the fighting.*

| Slot | Item | Key stats |
|------|------|-----------|
| Weapon | Rune or Staff | Survivability (Rune +HP) or mana pool (Staff +mana) |
| Hat | Hood | Skill + HP + mana (balanced defensive stats) |
| Robe | Mantle | HP + mana (stay alive) |

**Stat profile:** High HP, good mana, moderate skill, base speed.

**How it plays:** Your summons are your primary damage and your primary defense. Summon Golem
draws enemy aggro — while enemies attack your Golem, you Focus freely. Summoning spawns
minions that swarm enemies. Your weapon is secondary; you fire it between summon cooldowns
to contribute, but your creatures do the heavy lifting.

**Focus pattern:** Summon, then Focus behind your summons. The Golem is designed for this —
it takes aggro, buying you uninterrupted Focus time. When summons are active, Focus is nearly
free. When they're down, you're exposed.

**Spell picks:** Summon Golem (core — aggro tank), Summoning (core — damage minions), Healing
Idol (sustain for you and potentially summons), Barrier or Heal (backup survival for cooldown
gaps).

**Threat:** Cooldown gaps. Golem has a 30-second cooldown, Summoning 20 seconds. When your
summons die and cooldowns aren't ready, you're alone with mediocre personal damage and no
aggro shield. These windows are when you're most vulnerable — the build lives and dies by
cooldown management.

**Fantasy:** Summoner. The mage who commands an army.

## Idol Builder

*Set up your position. Make every bullet count double.*

| Slot | Item | Key stats |
|------|------|-----------|
| Weapon | Wand | Fast fire rate (more bullets through idol), homing |
| Hat | Headband or Hat | Skill + speed for repositioning, or pure skill |
| Robe | Robe or Vesture | Mana for sustained fire, or balanced stats |

**Stat profile:** High skill, good mana or speed, moderate HP.

**How it plays:** Place a Strength Idol or Numbers Idol, then fire your Wand through it. The
Wand's fast fire rate maximizes bullets-through-idol per second, and homing means bullets path
toward enemies after passing through. Your idol is your "base" — you position near it, fire
through it, and Focus beside it. Combat is about controlling a zone rather than chasing enemies.

**Focus pattern:** Positional. Set up idol, Focus near it, fire through it. The idol gives you
a reason to hold ground, turning Focus from "standing still because you have to" into "standing
still because this is where your power is." If enemies push you off your idol, you lose your
damage multiplier and need to reposition and replant.

**Spell picks:** Strength Idol (core — bullet damage boost), Numbers Idol (core — bullet
duplication), Blink (snap back to your idol if pushed away), Chain Lightning or Fireball
(filler damage when repositioning).

**Threat:** Mobile enemies. Skitters rush past your idol setup. Rangers fire from angles where
your idol isn't between you and them. Any enemy that forces you to abandon your position
neutralizes your core advantage. The 5-second idol cooldown helps with replanting, but you
lose uptime every time you move.

**Fantasy:** Turret mage. The mage who turns a patch of ground into a kill zone.

## Build comparison

| Archetype | Offensive stat | Defense | Focus risk | Focus frequency | Damage style |
|-----------|---------------|---------|------------|-----------------|-------------|
| Sniper | Skill | Lowest | Dangerous | Low | Burst (instant spells + weapon) |
| Kite Mage | Skill | Speed | Safe (distance) | Moderate | Sustained weapon fire while moving |
| Tank | Neither | HP | Safe (HP) | High | Weapon + Barrier sustain |
| Channeler | Mana | Moderate | Dangerous | High | Sustained channels (Laser, Charge Blast) |
| Summoner | Neither | HP | Safe (summons) | Moderate | Summon DPS |
| Idol Builder | Skill | Varies | Safe (zoning) | Moderate | Amplified weapon fire |

### The three core builds

The three intended builds map cleanly to weapon choice:

| Build | Weapon | Offensive stat | How it deals damage |
|-------|--------|---------------|---------------------|
| Glass cannon | Wand (+skill) | Skill | High skill scaling on weapon shots and instant spells |
| Mana build | Staff (+mana) | Mana | Deep mana pool fuels long channeled spell durations |
| Tank | Rune (+HP) | Neither | Survives to keep fighting; Barrier converts HP advantage to mana |

Utility (speed, Blink, Haste, Frost) benefits all three equally. Hybrid builds work through
Hood (skill+HP+mana) and Vesture (all four stats) — they can use both instant and channeled
spells, just not as effectively as a specialist.

Each archetype answers the Focus question differently:
- **Sniper:** Focus rarely, but pray nothing hits you when you do.
- **Kite Mage:** Outrun everything, Focus at safe distance.
- **Tank:** Focus wherever you want — you can take the hits.
- **Channeler:** Focus often and long — refilling the mana pool that powers your channels.
- **Summoner:** Let the Golem take aggro, Focus in peace.
- **Idol Builder:** Hold your ground near your idol, Focus at your power spot.
